Make.com vs Zapier: the real cost difference for Shopify

Zapier prices by task (one completed action step = one task). Make.com prices by operation (one module execution = one operation). A typical Shopify order automation — receive order, update inventory, send confirmation, fire CAPI event — runs 4 tasks on Zapier and 5 operations on Make. Despite using slightly more operations, Make is typically 3–10x cheaper because its per-unit pricing is dramatically lower.

The free tier comparison

Zapier's free plan includes 100 tasks/month and only 5 Zaps. Make.com's free plan includes 1,000 operations/month and 2 active scenarios. For most Shopify stores with under 300 orders/month, Make's free tier covers the entire automation stack. Zapier's free tier runs out in days at any meaningful order volume.

When to consider Zapier

Zapier's connector library is broader and its interface is simpler for non-technical users. If your automation requirements are very simple (under 5 steps, well-known apps), Zapier's ease of use may justify the higher cost. For anything involving Shopify webhooks, multi-step data routing, or API calls, Make.com is the better choice at every price tier.

Is Make.com cheaper than Zapier for Shopify?
In almost all cases, yes. Make.com's Core plan at £9/month supports 10,000 operations. Zapier's equivalent plan costs £73.50/month. For stores doing moderate automation volume the annual saving is typically £700–£3,000.
What is the difference between a Make operation and a Zapier task?
A Zapier task = one action step completed. A Make operation = one module execution. A multi-step automation in Zapier (trigger + 3 actions = 3 tasks) vs Make (trigger + 3 modules = 4 operations). Make typically costs less per equivalent workflow.
