Why GA4 always shows less revenue than Shopify

GA4 relies on the purchase event firing in the customer's browser on the order confirmation page. Three things prevent this: iOS privacy settings (which block third-party tracking), ad blockers (which intercept the GA4 tag), and users who close the tab or navigate away before the confirmation page fully loads. A 10–25% loss rate is typical without server-side tracking.

What your GA4 loss rate tells you

Loss rate under 5%Normal — no action needed

The fix: server-side GA4 tracking

Server-side GA4 tracking fires the purchase event from your server (via Make.com or a Cloudflare Worker) at the moment Shopify confirms the order — independent of what the browser does. This recovers 80–90% of previously lost GA4 purchase events and dramatically improves attribution accuracy for Google Ads optimisation.

Why does GA4 always show less revenue than Shopify?
GA4 relies on the purchase event firing in the customer's browser on the order confirmation page. Ad blockers, iOS privacy settings, slow connections, and users closing the tab before the page loads all cause this event to be missed. A 10–25% loss rate is typical without server-side tracking.
Is a 10% difference between GA4 and Shopify normal?
A gap of under 10% is generally acceptable and expected. 10–20% signals a tracking configuration issue worth fixing. Over 20% means significant ad attribution data is missing and campaign decisions are being made on incomplete data.
