When Shopify Plus is actually worth the cost

Shopify Plus at £2,300/month is significant overhead. The financial case rests on three levers: lower transaction fees (0.15–0.2% vs 0.2–0.5% on Advanced), checkout extensibility (UI Extensions enabling custom fields, dynamic upsells, and B2B-specific checkout experiences), and unlimited staff accounts. The fee saving alone justifies Plus only at very high revenue levels.

The financial break-even (with Shopify Payments)

At £650,000/month revenue, the transaction fee saving from Plus vs Advanced equals the plan cost difference. Below this level, Advanced plan is cheaper even if you factor in checkout customisation value. Above it, Plus saves money on fees alone — everything else is a bonus.

The non-financial case for Plus

Checkout UI Extensions allow modifications impossible on any other plan — post-purchase upsells natively in checkout, custom B2B pricing, tiered discounts at checkout, and custom form fields. For stores where checkout conversion is a primary optimisation lever, Plus can justify its cost at lower revenue levels based on conversion rate improvement alone.

At what revenue does Shopify Plus make financial sense?
Based on transaction fee savings alone, Shopify Plus breaks even against the Advanced plan at approximately £600,000–£650,000 per month revenue when using Shopify Payments. Below this, Advanced plan is cheaper.
Is Shopify Plus worth it for checkout customisation alone?
If checkout UI extensions (custom fields, dynamic upsells, B2B pricing) are central to your business model, Plus may be worth it at lower revenue levels. The financial break-even shifts based on the revenue impact of those features.
