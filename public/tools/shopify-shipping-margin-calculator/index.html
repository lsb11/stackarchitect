Why free shipping thresholds kill Shopify margins

Free shipping is the single most effective conversion rate lever for Shopify stores — but it is also the most common source of invisible margin erosion. Setting the threshold too low means subsidising shipping costs on orders where the product margin cannot cover them.

How to calculate your true break-even threshold

Your free shipping threshold must cover: the product cost (COGS), the actual shipping cost, and Shopify's transaction fee (0.2–2% depending on plan and payment gateway). A threshold set below this combined cost means every order above the threshold loses money on shipping.

The 2.5x rule of thumb

For most Shopify stores with average shipping costs of £3.50–£6.00, setting free shipping at 2–2.5x the average product cost provides enough margin buffer. A store with £15 average COGS and £4.50 average shipping should set free shipping at approximately £40–£45 to remain profitable after all fees.

How do I calculate the right free shipping threshold for Shopify?
Divide your average shipping cost + COGS by your target gross margin percentage. For most stores, the free shipping threshold should be set at 2–2.5x the average product cost to remain profitable after all fees.
Does offering free shipping increase Shopify conversion rates?
Yes — studies consistently show free shipping increases conversion rates by 15–30%. The goal is not to remove free shipping but to set the threshold high enough that the margin gain from higher AOV offsets the shipping cost.
