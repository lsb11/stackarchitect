When upgrading your Shopify plan actually saves money

Each Shopify plan tier charges lower transaction fees in exchange for a higher monthly subscription. The break-even point — where the fee saving exceeds the plan cost increase — depends on your revenue and whether you use Shopify Payments.

Key break-even thresholds (GBP, with Shopify Payments)

Basic → Shopify~£32,000/month revenue
Shopify → Advanced~£93,000/month revenue
Advanced → Plus~£650,000/month revenue

Without Shopify Payments

If you use a third-party payment gateway, the additional transaction fee (2% on Basic, 1% on Shopify, 0.5% on Advanced) means upgrading pays for itself at much lower revenue thresholds. Without Shopify Payments, Basic to Shopify break-even drops to approximately £8,000/month.

// FREQUENTLY_ASKED

When should I upgrade from Shopify Basic to Shopify?
In GBP, the break-even point with Shopify Payments is around £32,000/month revenue — at that level the fee saving from the lower transaction rate covers the plan cost increase. Without Shopify Payments it's around £8,000/month.
Is Shopify Plus worth it?
Shopify Plus at £2,300/month makes financial sense for stores doing over £500,000/month in revenue where the 0.2% transaction rate and checkout extensibility provide enough value. Below that threshold, Advanced plan offers better economics.
Does using Shopify Payments change the break-even calculation?
Significantly. With Shopify Payments, you're only saving on the additional transaction fee (0.5–2% depending on plan), not the payment processing rate. Without it, savings per upgrade are much larger.
