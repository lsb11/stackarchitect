The average Shopify store wastes £200–£600/month on replaceable apps

Most Shopify stores accumulate apps gradually — one for email, one for reviews, one for inventory, one for upsells — until monthly costs reach £300–£800. Many of these can be replaced with free alternatives or Make.com automations without any loss of core functionality.

The apps most commonly replaced for free

Email marketing (Klaviyo → Systeme.io), inventory management (Stocky → Google Sheets), product reviews (Yotpo → Shopify native), upsell apps (Rebuy → custom Liquid), and pop-up/email capture (Privy → Klaviyo forms or native) are the most commonly replaced with zero-cost alternatives. Together these often represent £200–£400/month of recoverable spend.

Apps that are harder to replace

Helpdesk software like Gorgias is difficult to replace because of its Shopify-native ticket integration and macro systems. Subscription billing apps like ReCharge have no free equivalent with equivalent reliability. These are typically worth keeping.

Which Shopify apps are easiest to replace for free?
Email marketing (Klaviyo → Systeme.io), inventory management (Stocky → Google Sheets), product reviews (Yotpo → native Shopify), and upsells (Rebuy → custom Liquid) are the most commonly replaceable with free alternatives.
What is the average monthly app spend for a Shopify store?
Mid-sized Shopify stores typically spend £200–£600/month on apps. Stores doing £100k+/month often spend over £1,000/month before auditing their stack.
Is Make.com actually free for Shopify automations?
Make.com's free plan includes 1,000 operations/month and 2 active scenarios. Most small to mid stores can run their core automations (abandoned cart, inventory alerts, order notifications) within the free tier.
