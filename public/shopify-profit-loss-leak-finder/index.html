Where Shopify store profit leaks most often

Most Shopify stores track revenue carefully but have poor visibility into true net profit. The four most common profit leaks — ranked by frequency and impact — are ad spend attribution inefficiency, COGS creep, returns rate erosion, and app fee accumulation.

What a healthy Shopify P&L looks like

Net margin 20%+Excellent — strong operational efficiency
Net margin 15–20%Healthy — typical for well-run DTC stores
Net margin 10–15%Warning — investigate the largest cost line
Net margin below 10%Critical — immediate action needed

The fastest fixes by cost category

App fees: audit and replace with free alternatives (often £150–£400/month recoverable). Ad spend: improve tracking to recover ROAS attribution. Returns: add better product photography and size guides. Shopify fees: check your plan break-even point using the plan upgrade calculator.

What is a good net profit margin for a Shopify store?
A net margin above 15% is considered healthy for most Shopify stores. Above 20% is excellent. Below 10% is a warning sign that one or more cost categories need immediate attention.
Are Shopify app fees eating into my margins?
For the average mid-sized Shopify store, app fees represent 1–4% of revenue. At £300–£600/month in app costs on a £15,000/month revenue store, that is 2–4% of net margin going to software — often replaceable with free alternatives.
